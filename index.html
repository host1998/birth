Hiring Pregnant Women: Challenges and Opportunities

Hiring pregnant women can present certain practical challenges such as the six-month maternity leave, logistics, and the mindset of both the public and the pregnant women themselves. Women seeking job changes while pregnant are not often approached by recruiters.

Despite this, there are instances where organizations hire pregnant women when the employee's skill set outweighs the anticipated time off. For example, a representative from Flipkart confirmed that the company does not have any specific policy regarding hiring pregnant women, adhering to equal employment opportunity principles.

However, in some organizations, workers cannot take maternity leave until they have been employed for 80 days. Even so, it is important for employers to recognize that women’s expertise can outweigh concerns about the number of days they will be absent from work.
